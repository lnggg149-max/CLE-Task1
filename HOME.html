PRE-HISTORY

Moments leading to this era

The Second Vatican Council, also known as Vatican II, 
was a pivotal event in the history of the Catholic Church. 
Here are some key moments leading up to the council:

January 25, 1959: 
Pope John XXIII announced the intention to summon a Council. 

June 5, 1960: 
Pope John established preparatory commissions to draft the Council's agenda. 

December 25, 1961: 
Pope John convoked the Council in the apostolic constitution Humanae Salutis. 

July 20, 1962: 
Invitations were sent to separated Christian Churches and Communities to send delegate-observers 
to the Council. 

October 11, 1962: 
The Second Vatican Ecumenical Council solemnly opened. 


These events set the stage for the council's four sessions, each lasting between 8 and 12 weeks, 
from 1962 to 1965, where the Church aimed to modernize its teachings 
and practices to better connect with the modern world.

Mission and Vision

What they wanted to achieve

VISION


The Council aimed to "freshen" the Churchâ€™s traditions to better connect with modern humanity, a process commonly referred to as aggiornamento. The vision involved: A "New Pentecost" and Springtime: Pope John XXIII expressed a desire for a "rejuvenation with greater vigour of the Body of Christ that is the Church". Returning to the Sources: A desire to reform the Church by returning to its foundations (scripture, liturgy, and early Church fathers).The Church as "People of God": Lumen Gentium shifted the view of the Church from a strictly hierarchical institution to a community of believers ("People of God") on a pilgrimage. Dialogical Approach: Instead of condemning the modern world, the Council sought to offer "the medicine of mercy" and enter into dialogue.

Mission

The Mission: Evangelization and Engagement The Council sought to move the Church toward a missionary stance, away from a "fortress" mentality. The core mission included: Evangelization: The Decree on Missionary Activity, Ad Gentes, emphasized that missionary work is fundamental to the entire Church. Engagement with the World: Gaudium et Spes, the Pastoral Constitution on the Church in the Modern World, called the Church to share the concerns and hopes of all people, particularly the poor. Christian Unity: Unitatis Redintegratio aimed to promote unity among Christians. Universal Call to Holiness: Lumen Gentium highlighted that all baptized individuals are called to holiness and have a part in the Church's mission

The Four Key Pillars

foundational documents known as Constitutions

Sacrosanctum Concilium

The Constitution on the Sacred Liturgy. It aimed to increase lay participation and allowed Mass to be celebrated in local languages instead of only Latin.

Lumen Gentium

The Dogmatic Constitution on the Church. It shifted the focus to the "Universal Call to Holiness" and defined the Church as the "People of God."

Dei Verbum

The Dogmatic Constitution on Divine Revelation. It encouraged Catholics to study the Bible deeply, viewing Scripture and Tradition as one sacred deposit of faith.

Gaudium et Spes

The Pastoral Constitution on the Church in the Modern World. It addressed social justice, human rights, and the Church's role in serving humanity.

Aggiornamento vs Ressourcement

The two engines that drove the council

Aggiornamento

"Bringing up to date"

Coined by Pope John XXIII, this Italian term describes the process of "updating" the Church's presentation of the faith. It focuses on translation and dialogue.

  • Focus: The Present & Future
  • Goal: To make the Gospel understandable to modern people.
  • Action: Vernacular liturgy and engagement with modern science.

Ressourcement

"Return to the sources"

This French term refers to a "retrieval" of the original spirit of Christianity found in the Bible and the writings of the Early Church Fathers.

  • Focus: The Origins & Tradition
  • Goal: To rediscover the living heart of faith beneath layers of custom.
  • Action: Renewed biblical study and Patristic theology.

The "Both/And" Paradox: The Church teaches that these two movements are inseparable. Without Ressourcement, updating becomes mere trend-following. Without Aggiornamento, returning to the sources becomes a museum-like obsession with the past.
